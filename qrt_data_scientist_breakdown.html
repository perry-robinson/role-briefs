1 What the Role Actually Does

In Plain English

This person is the data plumbing specialist for QRT's commodities trading desk. Their job is to take raw, messy data from a wide range of sources — think energy prices, shipping volumes, weather feeds, satellite imagery, and conventional market data — and turn it into clean, reliable, decision-ready datasets that researchers and traders can actually use to generate alpha.

They sit between the quantitative researchers who build trading models and the data engineers who manage the infrastructure. Think of them as a translator: they understand what the researchers need, know how to get the data from disparate sources, and can write the code to clean, transform, and structure it into something useful. They are not building trading strategies themselves — but without them, the strategies cannot be built.

On a daily basis this could involve writing Python scripts to ingest a new alternative data feed from a satellite provider, cleaning inconsistencies in an energy futures time series, working with an external vendor to understand a new dataset, or prototyping a pipeline to extract structured information from unstructured documents (PDFs, HTML pages, etc.). The commodities angle means they will handle a lot of physical market data — oil inventories, gas flows, crop reports — which is inherently messier than equity data.

3 Technology Stack Explained

Every Technology, Decoded

Python Core Language

The dominant programming language in quantitative finance for data work. Used to write every data pipeline, cleaning script, and transformation in this role. Think of it as the lingua franca of modern data science.

Pandas Library

A Python library for working with tabular data (like spreadsheets, but at scale). Used to load, filter, reshape, and analyse structured datasets — essential for any time series or market data manipulation.

NumPy Library

Python's core numerical computing library. Works under the hood of almost everything financial in Python. Used for high-speed mathematical operations on large arrays of numbers — price data, returns, etc.

Data Pipelines Concept

Automated workflows that move data from a raw source (say, an API or file dump) through cleaning and transformation steps, and into a final usable form. This candidate will design and maintain these for the commodities desk.

Alternative Data Data Type

Non-traditional financial data used to generate trading signals. In commodities this includes satellite imagery of oil tankers or crop fields, weather forecasts, shipping AIS data, social sentiment, and supply chain indicators. Extracting and cleaning this is significantly harder than standard market data.

Traditional Financial Data Data Type

Standard market data: prices, volumes, open interest, futures curves, and economic releases. Providers include Bloomberg, Refinitiv, ICE, and CME. The candidate needs comfort with both this and alternative data.

ETL / Data Onboarding Process

Extract, Transform, Load — the end-to-end process of getting a new dataset into a usable state within the firm's systems. The JD explicitly calls this out as a core responsibility, from initial evaluation of a dataset through to production use.

Data Extraction & Parsing Technique

Converting raw formats (PDFs, HTML pages, APIs, CSVs, XML feeds, databases) into structured data. In commodities this might mean pulling supply reports from government websites, parsing shipping manifests, or scraping exchange data.

5 Red Flags & Search Complexity

What to Watch Out For

⚠️
The ML Engineer Misfit

Many candidates will have "Data Scientist" on their CV but spend the majority of their time building machine learning models rather than data pipelines. QRT wants someone who is obsessed with data quality and engineering, not someone primarily focused on model building. Screen carefully for pipeline and ETL experience versus pure modelling work.

⚠️
No Financial Markets Interest

The JD explicitly calls out a "demonstrable interest in financial markets." This is not a generic data science role. Candidates from pure tech or non-finance verticals who cannot speak to markets will not clear the interview process regardless of technical quality.

⚠️
The Dual Location Complexity

This role is listed for both London and Aarhus. Aarhus is a very specific relocation requirement — a small Danish city with a limited local talent pool. Clarify upfront with the client which location is preferred and whether relocation support exists for London candidates, or whether these are genuinely separate headcount.

🔍
Search Complexity: Commodities Data Specialism

The commodities angle narrows the pool considerably. Equity and credit quant data scientists are far more numerous. Physical commodities data (energy flows, satellite, shipping AIS) is a niche within a niche. Expect a longer search and be prepared to consider candidates with strong data engineering skills in commodities even if their buy-side experience is limited.

🔍
Search Complexity: QRT's High Bar

QRT is a top-tier systematic fund with a notoriously rigorous hiring process. Candidates need both technical excellence and academic pedigree. Managing candidate expectations around process length and interview depth will be important. Average time to offer at firms like this is often 8 to 12 weeks.

6 Candidate Pitch

How to Sell This Role

"I wanted to reach out about a data science role that I think is genuinely different to most of what you will see on the market."

Open with the firm, not the title. QRT is one of the most respected systematic investment managers in the world. They have a serious reputation for intellectual rigour, strong performance, and genuine investment in their people. For a quantitative data scientist, this is a brand that carries real weight.

Sell the problem, not the job spec. This is not a role where you will be handed clean data and asked to build models. You will be solving genuinely hard data problems — extracting signal from some of the most complex and heterogeneous datasets in finance. Commodities data is messy, global, and often physical in nature. If you have ever wanted to work on satellite imagery of oil fields or AIS shipping data at a firm with the infrastructure and budget to actually do something with it, this is the opportunity.

Pitch the position in the firm. You sit at the intersection of research, trading, and engineering. Your work directly influences investment decisions. This is not a support function — the trading desk depends on what you deliver. There is high visibility and a clear line of sight between your output and firm performance.

Close with the practical. It is based in London (or Aarhus if relevant), the comp is competitive with top quant funds, and the team is small enough that your individual contribution matters. This is the kind of role that does not come up often — and when it does at a firm like QRT, it attracts serious attention.
